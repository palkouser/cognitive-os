# Experience assessment

Every timeline entry receives an evidence-linked status, correctness, necessity, efficiency, and
policy-compliance assessment. Unknown is a normal result when authoritative evidence is absent.
Acceptance, required verifiers, deterministic policy, and tool postconditions outrank provider
proposals.

The first observed failure and causal origin are separate. A policy denial proves that the denied
operation violated policy; it does not prove unrelated downstream causation. Failed verifiers and
tool postconditions identify objective observations, while their root cause may remain unknown.

Successful paths retain accepted entries and exact evidence. Failed branches are never discarded.
Corrections and recovery link before/after evidence without assuming that temporal proximity proves
effect. Contributions use only helpful, neutral, harmful, or unknown and never claim global model,
tool, skill, or strategy superiority. Generalizability is minimum-based; a single trajectory remains
task-specific.
